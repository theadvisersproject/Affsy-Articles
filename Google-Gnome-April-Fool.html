Google has recently introduced its another smart project "Google Gnome" which works with Netflix (only if you have a premium streaming membership). GNOME was originally an acronym for GNU Network Object Model Environment but the acronym was dropped because it no longer reflected the vision of the GNOME project. Anyway, from my views it worth a review, so let's get started. :)

Google Gnome - Outdoor and Indoor

Google Gnome outdoor is a kind of AI - more advanced than SiRi I guess. You can assign it tasks such as turning on grass cutter on your command. Or you can ask it about the weather today ! Or you can ask random questions such as " 'Nome, Can I eat a lemon tree leaf ?" Just anything ! You can do with ultra smart Gnome. [caption id="attachment_1197" align="aligncenter" width="385"] Google Gnome - Outdoor[/caption]   And the funniest part I loved about this product is that it is a nerd ! Yep ! Try asking him a simple thing, "Should I compose my wrapper of McDonalds ? " And it'll tell you all the scientific definitions including "we all are composable and if we compose it turns into an organic matter..." I am not going that deep though.. Anyway, The smart yard is here ! The Gnome indoor will be happily turn on your fan, air conditioner, or can play in-door music for you. And if you wanna watch some of your favorite TV shows, why not just ask it Gnome, tune up the show "Friends" and tada ! Have your smart indoor and outdoor with Google Gnome. For more information and a cool descriptive video visit the official website of Gnome.
