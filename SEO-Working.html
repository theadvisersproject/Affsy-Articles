People's behavior to search engine results

Now, here's the important part that most people just don't know, over 94% of people that use Google only clicks on results of this first page and if they don't find what they're looking for they subsequently don't bother clicking on more results instead they typically prefer to adjust their search terms and start from scratch. And for those people who do click on first page most only sometimes click on what referred to as the golden triangle or top 3 results on the first page. This is why, optimizing your websites for search engine or doing SEO is really necessary, if people search for something and they see it in your website, they click on your pages and visit your website to find the content they need. And these aren't just any visitors, these are people that are looking for exactly what you offer so of anyone they're most likely to convert into customers of yours. Regarding our example

So in our example we've searched for "small business marketing" and we get about 740 million results. It is not even a small number. Have you ever wondered why some websites show up on the first page and others get stuck on way down the bottom of pile? Well a site position in search results is determined by what's referred to as page rank. Every website has a ranking for particular search terms or keywords. In the beginning the first search engines worked by tracking all the words on pages, so if you search for small business marketing, you'd get back a list of websites containing those three keywords and the websites that had those words most often were ranked higher and they showed up towards the top live result pages. What Search Engines do now to rank your website? And how does SEO work?

Search engines still look at your website for keywords but they then rank your site's relevance on the number of quality backlinks to it from other websites, so think of it like this.. Keywords let's Google see your site and know what's its about. Backlinks determine how much Google trusts your site to be relevant. So keyword is important because they tell Google that you're out there but backlinks move your website up the stack.  That's why the search results you see are called organic or natural results. They're constantly changing based on what people actually do out there on web. Unsuprisingly, as there are two aspects to how search engines find and rank pages there are two corresponding components to SEO.

One. On-Site Component

This is where you ensure your site is full of keywords that are relevant to what your site's about. So for example, if you have a small business marketing website you want to make sure relevant keywords are on your site like, "business", "marketing", etc. things like that.  Two. Off-Site Component

This is where you work to create backlinks to your website that originate from other sources. Links to your site can create using articles, social media, blog posts, discussion boards and so on. But keep this in mind that most influential or legitimate the website is that links back to you, the more effect that backlink from that website is going to have on your search rankings.
